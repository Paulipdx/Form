FSA / HSA Tip

Letter of Medical Necessity

Many FSA/HSA plans require a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) to prove massage is treating a specific condition rather than general relaxation. Ask your physician for one before your first visit.

Clients with PPO plans may pay the cash rate upfront and submit a Superbill (with CPT codes 97124 or 97140) for partial reimbursement from:

  • UnitedHealthcare (UHC) — Choice Plus and top-tier PPO plans with medical necessity
  • Aetna — PPO plans; many require service inside a DC/PT office
  • Cigna — When documented as part of an active physical therapy plan
  • Kaiser PPO Plus — Self-referred rider covering up to 40% for ~12 visits/year
  • Providence Health Plan — Fully funded PPO plans with rigorous medical documentation
  • Regence BCBS / Anthem BCBS — Alternative care rider; fixed copay for 12–24 visits/year
  • Moda Health & PacificSource — "Plus" or "AHP" employer plans with dedicated massage benefits
  • Compass Rose (UHC) — Federal employees' High Option plan; no specialist referral required
  • TriWest / VA CCN — Fully covered when authorized by a VA physician
  • Medicare Advantage (Part C) — Select plans include therapeutic massage as a wellness benefit
  • The "Under Supervision" trap — Some plans require billing under a DC or PT NPI; independent LMTs see these claims denied
  • Deductible barriers — OON benefits don't pay until your full OON deductible is met for the year
  • Third-party administrators — eviCore and ASH often require prior authorization every 4–6 sessions

Questions about your coverage? Proactively verifying your benefits before your visit is essential. Contact us and we can provide a verification script for calling your insurance company or help you format a compliant Superbill template.
